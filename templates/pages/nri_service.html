Why It Matters

Risks of Managing Indian Property Alone

Without a trusted on-ground partner, NRI property ownership can quickly become a source of stress, financial loss, and legal liability.

Unverified Tenants Cost You
Placing tenants without proper background checks exposes you to non-payment, property damage, and long legal eviction battles. Our 7-point verification process — CIBIL, employment, identity, and references — protects your asset from the start.
NRI Tax Non-Compliance
Rental income is taxable in India regardless of where you live. Buyers of NRI properties must deduct TDS at up to 20%. Without expert guidance on TDS certificates and ITR filing, you risk significant penalties and blocked repatriation of funds.
Vacant Properties Deteriorate
An unoccupied property without routine maintenance depreciates rapidly. Water damage, encroachment, and unauthorized occupation are real risks. Our periodic inspection and maintenance coordination ensures your property retains its value and remains secure.
FEMA & RBI Compliance Gaps
Transactions involving NRI property are subject to FEMA regulations. Errors in repatriation, incorrect account types (NRE vs NRO), or missing RBI approvals can lead to legal complications. Our specialists ensure every transaction is fully compliant.

Everything NRIs need to know about managing Indian property from abroad.

Yes. NRIs can purchase residential and commercial property in India under FEMA guidelines without RBI permission. Agricultural land, plantation property, and farmhouses are excluded. Payments must be made through NRE/NRO accounts or home loan funds — foreign remittances are permitted. HOMEXO manages the entire buying process including title verification, RERA checks, and Sub-Registrar coordination.
When an NRI sells property, the buyer must deduct TDS at 20% (plus surcharge and cess) for long-term capital gains and 30% for short-term gains — applied on the full sale value, not just the gain. HOMEXO's tax team can apply for a Lower TDS Certificate under Section 197, which can dramatically reduce this deduction based on your actual taxable gain.
Rent is collected from tenants and transferred to your NRE (repatriable) or NRO (non-repatriable) bank account in India. From your NRE account, funds can be freely repatriated abroad. For NRO accounts, repatriation up to USD 1 million per financial year is permitted under RBI guidelines with a CA certificate. HOMEXO provides monthly rent statements and coordinates all transfers.
A Power of Attorney (POA) authorizes a trusted person in India to act on your behalf for property transactions — signing agreements, appearing at the Sub-Registrar Office, and handling government paperwork. For NRIs, a registered POA is usually essential for property registration. HOMEXO's advocates draft, notarize, and register your POA, including Apostille where required for foreign-executed documents.
