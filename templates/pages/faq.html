What documents do I need to buy a property in India?

For a residential purchase you'll typically need:

  • PAN Card and Aadhaar Card (identity + address proof)
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Bank statements (last 6 months) for loan applicants
  • Income proof (salary slips / ITR for 2–3 years)
  • Property-related documents (sale deed, approved plan, OC/CC)

Our legal team will guide you through every document required for your specific transaction.

What is a sale agreement and why is it important?

A sale agreement (also called Agreement for Sale) is a legally binding contract between buyer and seller that outlines the terms of the transaction — price, payment schedule, possession date, and default clauses. It precedes the final sale deed and protects both parties. HOMEXO's legal team can draft and review your sale agreement. Learn more →

What is stamp duty and who pays it?

Stamp duty is a state government tax paid on the registration of the sale deed. Rates vary by state (typically 5–8% of the property value). As the buyer, you are responsible for paying stamp duty and registration charges. HOMEXO calculates the exact stamp duty applicable to your transaction before you proceed.

Can NRIs buy residential property in India?

Yes. NRIs and PIOs can purchase residential and commercial properties in India under FEMA regulations without special RBI permission. They cannot buy agricultural land, plantation property, or farmhouse. HOMEXO has a dedicated NRI desk that handles power of attorney, NRO/NRE fund transfers, repatriation, and tax compliance. What checks should I do before buying an under-construction property?

  • Check the developer's track record and delivery history

  • Confirm clear land title and absence of encumbrance
  • Review approved building plan from local authority

What is capital gains tax on property sale?

If you sell a property held for more than 2 years, you pay Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax at 20% with indexation. Short-term (held under 2 years) gains are taxed at your income slab rate. You can save LTCG tax by reinvesting in another property (Section 54) or in NHAI/REC bonds (Section 54EC) within specified timeframes. Our team can connect you with a tax consultant.

We also account for the property's condition, floor, view, and amenities. This gives you a realistic price range that attracts serious buyers without underselling.

Do I need an OC (Occupancy Certificate) to sell my property?

While not always a legal requirement for sale, most mortgage lenders require the OC before approving a home loan for the buyer, which significantly narrows your buyer pool. RERA-registered properties must have an OC. HOMEXO's legal team can advise on your specific situation and help obtain outstanding documentation if needed.

Renting a Property

What is the difference between a leave-and-licence agreement and a lease?

A Leave and Licence (L&L) agreement is the most common residential rental agreement in India (typically 11 months). It creates a licence to occupy — not tenancy rights — making it easier for owners to recover possession. A lease grants stronger occupancy rights to the tenant for a fixed period and is more commonly used in long-term commercial arrangements. HOMEXO prepares both types of agreements through its legal team.

Is security deposit refundable?

Yes. The security deposit — typically 2–6 months' rent depending on the city — is fully refundable at the end of the tenancy, subject to deductions for damage (beyond normal wear and tear) and outstanding dues. Your rental agreement should clearly specify the deposit refund timeline (usually 15–30 days after vacating).

What should I check before signing a rental agreement?
  • Monthly rent and annual escalation percentage
  • Security deposit amount and refund conditions
  • Lock-in period duration
  • Notice period for vacating (typically 1–3 months)
  • Maintenance charges — who pays what
  • Pet and subletting policies

What is the maximum loan-to-value (LTV) ratio for home loans in India?

As per RBI guidelines:

  • Up to ₹30 lakh: max 90% LTV
  • ₹30–75 lakh: max 80% LTV
  • Above ₹75 lakh: max 75% LTV

This means you'll need to arrange the balance (down payment) from own sources. Stamp duty and registration charges are generally not financed and must be paid separately.

What is a fixed vs. floating interest rate home loan?

A fixed rate loan has the same EMI throughout the tenure — predictable but typically slightly higher. A floating rate loan (linked to RBI repo rate or MCLR) changes when rates change — lower when rates fall, higher when they rise. Most borrowers currently prefer floating rates given the current rate environment. HOMEXO's loan advisors will recommend the right mix for your situation.

How long does home loan approval take?

With complete documentation, most HOMEXO clients receive in-principle approval within 24–48 hours and full sanction within 3–5 working days. Disbursement typically happens within 7–14 days of sanction, depending on the bank and property documentation completeness. Our advisors track your application daily and resolve queries before they cause delays.

Can I prepay or foreclose my home loan without penalty?

For floating rate home loans taken by individual borrowers, the RBI has mandated zero prepayment penalties. Fixed rate loans may have a 2–3% foreclosure charge, which varies by lender. HOMEXO ensures the loan terms around prepayment are clearly explained before you sign.

What tax benefits are available on home loans?
  • Section 80C: Deduction up to ₹1.5 lakh per year on principal repayment
  • Section 24(b): Deduction up to ₹2 lakh per year on interest paid (self-occupied property)
  • Section 80EEA: Additional ₹1.5 lakh deduction on interest for first-time buyers (subject to conditions)

These deductions are available under the old tax regime. Consult a CA for guidance specific to your situation.

Legal Services

What is title verification and why is it essential?

Title verification is a legal search of the property's ownership history (typically last 30 years) to confirm the seller has a clear, marketable title and no encumbrances (mortgages, disputes, or court orders). It is the single most important legal step before buying any property. HOMEXO's empanelled lawyers complete this with a written opinion within 48 hours. Learn more →

What is an Encumbrance Certificate (EC)?

An EC is an official government document (from the Sub-Registrar's office) listing all registered transactions on a property for a specified period — mortgages, sale deeds, gift deeds, etc. A clean EC with no encumbrances indicates the property is free of legal or financial liabilities. Banks require a current EC before approving any home loan.

What is RERA and how does it protect buyers?

The Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA) mandates that all residential projects above 500 sqm or 8 apartments must be registered with the state's RERA authority. RERA requires developers to publish project details, timelines, and carpet area clearly, hold 70% of collections in an escrow account, and compensate buyers for delays. HOMEXO only recommends RERA-registered projects to buyers.

What is Power of Attorney (PoA) and when do I need it?

A Power of Attorney is a legal document authorising another person (the attorney) to act on your behalf for specified transactions. NRIs frequently use a notarised + apostilled PoA to complete property transactions in India without being physically present. HOMEXO assists with PoA preparation, attestation guidance, and coordination with local authorities.
