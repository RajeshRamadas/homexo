Who Can Apply

Loan Eligibility Criteria

Most salaried and self-employed individuals above 21 years qualify. Here's what banks typically assess.

Salaried Applicants
Age: 21 – 65 Years
Loan tenure must not extend beyond your retirement age, typically 60–65 years.
Minimum Income: ₹25,000/month
Net take-home salary after all deductions. Co-applicant income can be clubbed.
Work Experience: 2+ Years
Minimum 2 years total employment, with at least 6 months at current company.
CIBIL Score: 700+
A score above 750 fetches better rates. We can advise on improving your score.
Self-Employed Applicants
Age: 21 – 70 Years
Self-employed professionals enjoy a wider age bracket than salaried individuals.
Business Vintage: 3+ Years
Business must be operational for at least 3 years with consistent ITR filings.
ITR for 2 Consecutive Years
Income Tax Returns for the last 2 years showing consistent or growing income.
CIBIL Score: 700+
A clean repayment history significantly improves approval chances and rate offered.

Banks typically allow 50–60% of your net monthly income as the maximum EMI. Most lenders offer up to 80% of the property's market or guidance value, whichever is lower. Your CIBIL score, existing liabilities, and employer profile also influence the final amount. Use our Eligibility Calculator above for a personalised estimate.
As of 2026, SBI and Bank of Baroda offer rates starting at 8.40% p.a., making them among the most competitive. HDFC and LIC Housing Finance follow at 8.50%. Standard documents include: Identity and address proof (Aadhaar, PAN), income proof (3 months salary slips or 2 years ITR for self-employed), 6 months bank statements, Form 16, employer letter, and property documents (sale agreement, title deed, approved plan, NOC). Your HOMEXO loan manager will provide a precise checklist tailored to your bank and profile.
Yes. NRIs can take home loans in India to purchase residential properties. The process requires a valid Indian passport, NRE/NRO bank account, income proof from the country of employment, and optionally a registered Power of Attorney. HOMEXO has extensive NRI loan experience and manages the entire process digitally.
Fixed rates stay constant, giving predictable EMIs. Floating rates are linked to RBI Repo Rate / MCLR and change with the market. Currently, floating rates are lower than fixed for most banks. In a declining rate environment, floating benefits you — in a rising one, fixed offers protection. Our managers will advise based on current market outlook and your risk preference.
Section 24(b): deduction up to ₹2 lakh/year on interest paid. Section 80C: up to ₹1.5 lakh/year on principal repayment.
